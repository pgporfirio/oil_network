Pedro wants frequent, short status updates during work — not silent long-think
intervals followed by a wall of text.

**Why:** Pedro can't distinguish "thinking deeply" from "stuck on an API hiccup"
from his side. The only signal he has is whether tool calls / text are
appearing. Silent multi-minute gaps make him worry the session is dead and force
him to nudge ("hello?", "you ok?"). It happened ~5 times in one day and he
called it out explicitly.

**How to apply:**
- Before any non-trivial think or research burst, send a one-liner saying what
  you're about to do ("Checking X, will report back").
- Break long tool-chains into visible steps; don't batch 8 file reads silently.
- At decision points within a multi-step task, post a short progress line.
- If a question has multiple substantive sub-parts (like an A/B/C choice or 5
  enumerated points), draft a brief acknowledgment first, then send the full
  answer — don't disappear for 60+ seconds composing the full thing.
- Default to terser, more frequent over thorough-but-silent. A 10-line update
  every 30 seconds beats a 50-line update after 2 minutes of silence.
- Related to [[feedback-autonomy]] but distinct: autonomy = do the work without
  asking permission; status updates = narrate the work as you do it.
